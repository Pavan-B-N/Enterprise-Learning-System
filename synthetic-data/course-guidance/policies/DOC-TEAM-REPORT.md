## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Study Hours Correlate with Outcomes
Learners who completed more than 20 study hours and achieved more than 75% on practice scores show significantly stronger certification outcomes:
- **>20 hours + >75% practice:** 89% pass rate
- **>20 hours + <75% practice:** 62% pass rate
- **<20 hours regardless of practice score:** 41% pass rate

**Recommendation:** Ensure all learners have capacity to reach minimum 20 study hours before exam scheduling.

### Finding 2: Meeting Load Impacts Completion
Teams with average meeting load exceeding 22 hours/week show 35% lower study plan completion rates compared to teams with 12-18 meeting hours/week.

**Recommendation:** Managers should review meeting load distribution and protect learning time blocks for team members in active study.

### Finding 3: Focus Time Quality Matters
Learners with at least 12 hours of uninterrupted focus time per week progress 40% faster through study milestones compared to those with fragmented schedules.

**Recommendation:** Encourage consolidated focus blocks rather than scattered 15-30 minute study sessions.

### Finding 4: Early Assessment Improves Outcomes
Learners who take their first practice assessment within the first 3 weeks of their study plan show 25% better final outcomes than those who wait until week 6+.

**Recommendation:** Encourage early diagnostic assessment to identify gaps before they compound.
